Travel Information

Before your journey to Nigeria, here’s everything you need to know about visas, safety, transportation, currency, and essential travel tips.

Visa Requirements

Most international travelers require a visa to enter Nigeria.

Steps to apply:

  • Apply online through the Nigeria Immigration Service portal.
  • Choose the correct visa type: tourism, business, study, or transit.
  • Upload passport, photo, and payment confirmation.
  • Schedule a biometric appointment at your nearest embassy or consulate.
Tip: Your passport must have at least 6 months validity.

Safety Tips

Nigeria is a vibrant and welcoming country. As with any major destination, travelers should take normal safety precautions:

  • Use registered taxis or ride sharing apps like Bolt or Uber.
  • Keep valuables secure and avoid isolated areas late at night.
  • Stay informed about local news and government advisories.
  • Follow local customs and dress modestly in conservative regions.

Transportation

Nigeria offers a variety of transportation options:

  • Domestic flights Fastest way to travel between major cities.
  • Ride sharing apps Convenient for city travel.
  • Buses and minibuses Affordable public transport.
  • Car rentals Available at airports and major hotels.
Lagos and Abuja have well connected road networks, making city exploration easier.

Money & Currency

Nigeria’s official currency is the Naira (₦).

Useful tips:

  • Many places accept cash only, especially rural areas.
  • ATMs are widely available in major cities.
  • Credit cards work in hotels, malls, and large restaurants.
  • Always exchange money at authorized banks or bureau de change.
